The Impacts

The history of the Evangelion universe is defined by a series of catastrophic events called "Impacts," each representing a collision between humanity and forces far beyond its comprehension. First Impact — Approximately 4 billion years ago, a progenitor entity known as the Black Moon crashlanded on Earth, seeding it with the Seed of Life: Lilith. This event is hypothesized to be the origin of all life on Earth, including humans (Lilim).

Second Impact — September 13, 2000. The official story describes a meteorite impact in Antarctica. The truth: a scientific expedition to the South Pole made contact with Adam, the First Angel, triggering a cataclysmic explosion. The resulting floods and wars killed over 3 billion people and permanently shifted Earth's axial tilt, eliminating the seasons.

Third Impact — The goal of SEELE's Human Instrumentality Project: a controlled Third Impact that would dissolve all individual human consciousness into a collective, transcending the suffering of the human condition entirely.

The Angels (Messengers)

Angels — known formally as "Messengers" — are entities derived from Adam, the First Seed of Life. They possess massive power, unique physical forms, and are driven by an instinct to return to Adam (or Lilith) and trigger a catastrophic impact.

Each Angel is classified with a numerical designation corresponding to the order of their appearance. They bear names drawn from Judeo-Christian and Kabbalistic tradition.

  • Sachiel (3rd Angel) — The first Angel to appear in the series. Humanoid in form, with cross-shaped explosions. Defeated by Unit-01 in Episode 02.
  • Ramiel (5th Angel) — A massive geometric octahedron with a devastating particle beam cannon and an impenetrable AT Field. Defeated by Operation Yashima.
  • Leliel (12th Angel) — Exists as a shadow — a Dirac Sea pocket dimension — capable of swallowing an Eva whole. Defeated when Unit-01 went berserk.
  • Kaworu Nagisa (17th Angel) — The final Angel. Appears in human form and is the only Angel shown to communicate and empathize with humans. Designated Tabris.

AT Fields (Absolute Terror Fields)

AT Fields are the primary defensive mechanism of both Angels and Evangelions. In physical terms, they are force fields of immense strength, capable of repelling nearly any conventional weapon.

Philosophically, the series reveals that AT Fields are the physical manifestation of the human soul's individuality — the barrier between one consciousness and another. Every human possesses a weak AT Field; the pain of loneliness and the inability to truly connect with others is described as the cost of having one.

"AT Field — the light of the soul. The sacred domain that no one may invade." — Ritsuko Akagi

The Evangelion Units

Evangelions are not machines — they are living beings. Each unit is constructed around the soul of a person, typically the pilot's mother, which serves as its "core." This is why only specific children — born after the Second Impact — are able to synchronize with the units.

  • Unit-00 (Prototype) — Pilot: Rei Ayanami. Blue. The first functional Eva; considered unstable in early tests. Contains the soul of Rei's "original."
  • Unit-01 (Test Type) — Pilot: Shinji Ikari. Purple. The most plot-central Eva. Contains the soul of Yui Ikari, Shinji's mother, who was absorbed into it during an accident.
  • Unit-02 (Production Model) — Pilot: Asuka Langley Soryu. Red. Manufactured in Germany. Contains the fragmented soul of Asuka's mother, Kyoko.
  • Unit-03 (Production Model) — Pilot: Toji Suzuhara. Black. Infected by the 13th Angel (Bardiel) during transport from the United States.

The Magi Supercomputer System

The Magi is a trio of supercomputers that form the decision-making backbone of NERV. Designed by Dr. Naoko Akagi and later maintained by her daughter Ritsuko, the Magi system is unique in that it was built around three aspects of Naoko Akagi's own personality, preserved digitally:

  • Magi-01 (Melchior) — Naoko as a scientist. Prioritizes empirical data and logical outcomes.
  • Magi-02 (Balthasar) — Naoko as a mother. Weighs outcomes against human cost and emotional consequence.
  • Magi-03 (Caspar) — Naoko as a woman. The most unpredictable of the three; driven by personal desire.
