// Overview

What is Neon Genesis Evangelion?

Original Run: 1995–1996  |  Studio: Gainax  |  Director: Hideaki Anno

Neon Genesis Evangelion (新世紀エヴァンゲリオン) is a landmark Japanese animated series created by Hideaki Anno. Set in a post-apocalyptic Tokyo-3, it follows teenager Shinji Ikari, who is recruited by his estranged father Gendo to pilot a massive biomechanical robot known as an Evangelion Unit to combat catastrophic beings called Angels.

The Second Impact

Event Date: September 13, 2000  |  Classification: Global Catastrophe

On September 13, 2000, an event known as the Second Impact struck Antarctica, melting its ice caps and triggering global floods and wars that killed half of Earth's population. The official story blames a meteorite, but the truth is far more disturbing — and buried deep within the files of SEELE and NERV.

Tokyo-3: The Last Fortress

Location: Hakone, Japan  |  Status: Geofront Capital

Tokyo-3 is the fortified city built over NERV headquarters and the Geofront — a massive underground cavity housing the Evangelion production facilities. When Angels attack, the entire city can retract into the ground, leaving only defensive structures exposed. It is humanity's last line of defense.

Did you know? The name "Evangelion" comes from the Greek word εὐαγγέλιον (euangélion), meaning "good news" or "gospel." The series draws heavily from Jewish and Christian mysticism, Kabbalistic texts, and Jungian psychology.
