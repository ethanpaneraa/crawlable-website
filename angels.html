Angel Bestiary

A complete catalog of all seventeen Angels encountered during the series, their forms, abilities, and fates.

Classification note: Angels are numbered by order of appearance, not by order of creation. The First and Second Angels — Adam and Lilith — are the progenitor Seeds of Life from which all others descend. NERV designates combat-encountered Angels starting from the Third.

// Progenitors

Adam — 1st Angel

Status: Reduced  |  Location: Terminal Dogma (formerly Antarctica)

The First Seed of Life, Adam arrived on Earth aboard the White Moon billions of years ago. Contact with Adam during the Katsuragi Expedition in 2000 triggered the Second Impact. By the time of the series, Adam has been reduced to an embryonic state and is kept in the possession of Gendo Ikari, fused into his right hand. All Angels — except the Children of Lilith — are descended from Adam.

Lilith — 2nd Angel

Status: Crucified  |  Location: Terminal Dogma, NERV HQ

The Second Seed of Life, Lilith arrived aboard the Black Moon — the object that would become the Geofront. She is crucified deep within Terminal Dogma, impaled by the Lance of Longinus, and drained of her blood (LCL), which fills the blood-red sea surrounding her. All humans — Lilim — are her descendants. NERV falsely labels her as "Adam" to conceal her true identity from most personnel.

// Combat Angels — 3rd through 17th

  • 03

    Sachiel — "Water of God"

    First Appearance: Episode 01  |  Status: Destroyed

    The first Angel encountered after the Second Impact. Humanoid in form, with a distinctive bone mask and cross-shaped energy explosions. Sachiel demonstrated the ability to regenerate mid-battle and briefly overpowered Unit-01 before being destroyed when Unit-01 went berserk for the first time.

  • 04

    Shamshel — "Sun of God"

    First Appearance: Episode 03  |  Status: Destroyed

    An elongated, centipede-like Angel armed with two whip-like energy tentacles capable of slicing through armor. Shamshel was defeated by Shinji piloting solo, though his emotional state during the battle caused significant collateral damage to the surrounding city.

  • 05

    Ramiel — "Thunder of God"

    First Appearance: Episode 05  |  Status: Destroyed

    A massive, constantly shifting geometric octahedron — one of the most visually iconic Angels in the series. Ramiel was armed with an enormously powerful positron beam cannon and a perfectly spherical AT Field impenetrable by conventional means. It required Operation Yashima — draining Japan's entire power grid — to defeat it with a single long-range positron sniper shot.

  • 06

    Gaghiel — "Fish of God"

    First Appearance: Episode 08  |  Status: Destroyed

    An aquatic Angel that attacked a UN fleet in the Pacific Ocean during Unit-02's transport to Japan. Enormous in size, Gaghiel swallowed two battleships whole. Asuka and Shinji, piloting Unit-02 jointly aboard the aircraft carrier, defeated it by prying open its mouth and firing the fleet's guns directly into its core from inside.

  • 07

    Israfel — "Song of God"

    First Appearance: Episode 09  |  Status: Destroyed

    An Angel capable of splitting into two equal, independently operating bodies — designated Alpha and Beta. A joint assault by Shinji and Asuka initially failed when they destroyed only one half and the other regenerated its twin. After two weeks of strict synchronization training, the two pilots defeated both halves simultaneously in a perfectly choreographed attack.

  • 08

    Sandalphon — "Co-Brother of God"

    First Appearance: Episode 10  |  Status: Destroyed

    Discovered in a dormant, embryonic state inside an active volcano. NERV attempted to capture it alive for study before it hatched, but the embryo awakened prematurely. Asuka, piloting Unit-02 in a specially cooled entry plug, dove into the magma and killed Sandalphon with a progressive knife before it could fully emerge.

  • 09

    Matarael — "Rain of God"

    First Appearance: Episode 11  |  Status: Destroyed

    A spider-like Angel that attacked during a city-wide blackout, disabling NERV's systems entirely. Matarael dripped a powerful acidic fluid from an eye in its abdomen, burning through Nerv HQ's armor. The three pilots defeated it manually in the dark using backup weapons and improvised coordination with no HQ support.

  • 10

    Sahaquiel — "Wit of God"

    First Appearance: Episode 12  |  Status: Destroyed

    A massive orbital Angel the size of a small city, Sahaquiel fell from space toward Tokyo-3 in a controlled descent, using its own body as a kinetic weapon. All three Evangelion units caught it bare-handed mid-fall and tore out its core — one of the most visually spectacular battles in the series and a rare moment of genuine teamwork between Shinji, Rei, and Asuka.

  • 11

    Iruel — "Fear of God"

    First Appearance: Episode 13  |  Status: Destroyed

    Unlike any other Angel, Iruel had no physical body — it existed as a microscopic colony organism that infected NERV's computer systems and spread like a virus. It rapidly evolved to counter every defensive measure Ritsuko deployed, eventually gaining partial control of the Magi. Ritsuko defeated it by rewriting Caspar's base programming to force a self-rejection response.

  • 12

    Leliel — "Shadow of God"

    First Appearance: Episode 16  |  Status: Destroyed

    One of the most conceptually disturbing Angels. Leliel appeared as a floating black-and-white sphere but was in fact an inverted Dirac Sea — a pocket dimension. The "sphere" was merely a shadow; the real body was the flat, pattern-less disc beneath it. It swallowed Unit-01 whole, trapping Shinji inside for hours while a manifestation of his inner self confronted him. Unit-01 tore its way free by going berserk.

  • 13

    Bardiel — "Lightning of God"

    First Appearance: Episode 18  |  Status: Destroyed

    Bardiel infected Evangelion Unit-03 during its transport from the United States, using the Eva itself as its vessel. This forced NERV into the most ethically catastrophic decision of the series: Gendo activated the Dummy System in Unit-01, which — controlled by Rei's data rather than Shinji — destroyed Unit-03 with Toji Suzuhara still inside the entry plug. Shinji watched, helpless, unable to override the autopilot.

  • 14

    Zeruel — "Arm of God"

    First Appearance: Episode 19  |  Status: Destroyed (partially absorbed)

    Among the most powerful Angels in the series, Zeruel breached NERV HQ directly. Its paper-thin foil-like arms could slice through Eva armor effortlessly, and it destroyed Unit-00 and Unit-02 before reaching Central Dogma. Shinji, having returned to pilot after quitting, achieved a sync rate so extreme Unit-01 activated without power and consumed Zeruel to restore itself — an event classified as a "pseudo-Third Impact" precursor.

  • 15

    Arael — "Light of God"

    First Appearance: Episode 22  |  Status: Neutralized (Lance of Longinus)

    An angelic, bird-like entity that remained in high orbit, unreachable by the Evangelions. Rather than attacking physically, Arael projected a powerful mind-invasive beam at Unit-02 that forced Asuka to relive her most traumatic memories — her mother's breakdown, suicide, and the horrifying discovery that Kyoko's soul had not entered Asuka but a doll. Rei used the Lance of Longinus to neutralize it from the surface, losing the Lance permanently in the process.

  • 16

    Armisael — "Ring of God"

    First Appearance: Episode 23  |  Status: Destroyed (self-detonation)

    A circular, double-helix Angel that attempted to merge with Evangelion units at the cellular level. After partially fusing with Unit-00 and beginning to merge with Unit-01, Rei made the decision to self-detonate Unit-00's core — killing herself and destroying Armisael in the explosion. This was Rei's second death in the series; she was later replaced by Rei III.

  • 17

    Tabris (Kaworu Nagisa) — "Free Will of God"

    First Appearance: Episode 24  |  Status: Destroyed (by Shinji's choice)

    The final Angel and the most extraordinary. Tabris arrived in human form as Kaworu Nagisa, the Fifth Child. He was the sole Angel to possess genuine empathy, free will, and the capacity for love — qualities he directed toward Shinji in the brief time they knew each other. Descending to Terminal Dogma, Kaworu chose to surrender himself rather than trigger Third Impact, asking Shinji to kill him. Shinji complied. Kaworu is the only Angel the series mourns.

On Angel anatomy: Every Angel possesses a core — a red sphere typically located in the chest — which is the seat of its life. Destroying the core is the only reliable method of killing an Angel. All Angels also generate AT Fields of varying strength and structure; neutralizing an Angel's AT Field is a prerequisite to reaching its core in most combat scenarios.
