Episode Guide

Complete episode listing for the original Neon Genesis Evangelion television series (1995–1996).

// Part I — Angel Attacks

  • 01

    Angel Attack

    Air Date: October 4, 1995

    Fourteen-year-old Shinji Ikari arrives in Tokyo-3 and is immediately caught in a battle between the military and a gigantic creature called an Angel. His father Gendo recruits him to pilot the mysterious Evangelion Unit-01.

  • 02

    The Beast

    Air Date: October 11, 1995

    After being injured in battle, Shinji awakens to find Unit-01 has been acting on its own. He begins to settle into life in Tokyo-3 and moves in with NERV operations director Misato Katsuragi.

  • 03

    A Transfer

    Air Date: October 18, 1995

    Shinji begins attending school and befriends Toji Suzuhara and Kensuke Aida, despite a rocky start. A new Angel appears, and Shinji must fight in Unit-01 while his classmates are trapped inside the entry plug.

  • 04

    Hedgehog's Dilemma

    Air Date: October 25, 1995

    Feeling isolated and overwhelmed, Shinji runs away from NERV. He wanders Tokyo-3 and reflects on his inability to connect with others — a recurring theme the series terms the "Hedgehog's Dilemma."

  • 05

    Rei I

    Air Date: November 1, 1995

    Shinji visits Rei's apartment on an errand from his father. An Angel called Ramiel — a massive geometric shape with a powerful beam cannon — arrives and delivers NERV's worst defeat yet.

  • 06

    Rei II

    Air Date: November 8, 1995

    NERV launches Operation Yashima, a precision sniping attack on Ramiel using Japan's full electrical grid. Rei and Shinji work together in what becomes one of the series' most iconic moments.

  • 07

    A Human Work

    Air Date: November 15, 1995

    NERV is bypassed when a government agency deploys its own giant robot — Jet Alone — powered by nuclear energy. The machine goes rogue, and Misato must intervene before a meltdown destroys a city.

  • 08

    Asuka Strikes!

    Air Date: November 22, 1995

    The Second Child, Asuka Langley Soryu, arrives from Germany along with Unit-02. She immediately takes charge as a new Angel appears, forcing her and Shinji to fight in perfect synchronization aboard an aircraft carrier.

  • 09

    Both of You, Dance Like You Want to Win!

    Air Date: November 29, 1995

    An Angel capable of splitting into two separate bodies forces Shinji and Asuka to synchronize their movements — literally — and they must train together by living in the same apartment for two weeks.

  • 10

    Magmadiver

    Air Date: December 6, 1995

    An unhatched Angel is discovered dormant inside a volcano. Asuka dives into the magma in Unit-02 to retrieve it before it can hatch, in one of the series' more action-focused and light-hearted episodes.

  • 11

    The Day Tokyo-3 Stood Still

    Air Date: December 27, 1995

    A power outage plunges Tokyo-3 into darkness, disabling NERV's systems as an Angel attacks. The pilots must fight in the dark, relying on manual controls and instinct — with no support from HQ.

  • 12

    She Said, "Don't Make Others Suffer for Your Personal Hatred."

    Air Date: January 3, 1996

    Misato's complicated feelings about the Angels — rooted in her survival of the Second Impact — come to a head during a battle. Shinji and Asuka defeat the 8th Angel Sahaquiel, a massive organism falling from orbit, in a rare moment of teamwork and trust.

  • 13

    Lilliputian Hitcher

    Air Date: January 10, 1996

    An Angel infiltrates the Magi supercomputer network as a computer virus, threatening to detonate NERV HQ from within. Ritsuko races to stop it before it achieves a 2-of-3 Magi consensus to self-destruct the base.

  • 14

    Weaving a Story 2: Oral Stage

    Air Date: January 17, 1996

    A clip-and-recap episode that reviews the series through the lens of SEELE's evaluation of the pilots. Notably, Rei and Shinji are asked to cross-synchronize with each other's units — Rei in Unit-01, Shinji in Unit-00 — with disturbing results.

  • 15

    Those Women Longed for the Touch of Others' Lips, and Thus Invited Their Kisses

    Air Date: January 24, 1996

    A quieter, character-focused episode. Shinji visits his mother's grave with his father — one of the few times they interact without hostility. Misato and Kaji rekindle their relationship. Rei begins to question her own sense of self and what it means to exist.

// Part II — Psychological Descent

  • 16

    Splitting of the Breast

    Air Date: January 31, 1996

    Shinji is swallowed by the Angel Leliel, which exists as a shadow — a pocket dimension. Trapped inside, he confronts a manifestation of his inner self and his deepest fears about acceptance and identity.

  • 18

    Ambivalence

    Air Date: February 14, 1996

    Toji is revealed as the Fourth Child and activates Unit-03, which has been infected by an Angel. Gendo deploys the Dummy System — an autopilot running on Rei's data — and Shinji watches in horror as Unit-01 brutally destroys Unit-03 with Toji inside.

  • 19

    Introjection

    Air Date: February 21, 1996

    Devastated by the Dummy System incident, Shinji quits piloting. During a subsequent Angel attack, Unit-01 awakens on its own — berserk — and destroys the Angel with terrifying primal violence, ending in Unit-01 feeding on the Angel to restore its power.

// Part III — The End

  • 24

    The Beginning and the End, or Knockin' on Heaven's Door

    Air Date: March 13, 1996

    The seventeenth Angel, Kaworu Nagisa, arrives as the Fifth Child. Kaworu and Shinji develop a deep connection in a short time — but Kaworu's true nature is revealed when he descends to Terminal Dogma, and Shinji is forced to make a devastating choice.

  • 25

    Do You Love Me?

    Air Date: March 20, 1996

    As SEELE initiates the Human Instrumentality Project, the final two episodes abandon conventional animation and enter an expressionistic, introspective space. Shinji faces psychological evaluation within his own mind, questioning the value of his existence.

  • 26

    Take Care of Yourself

    Air Date: March 27, 1996

    The series' controversial finale. Inside his mind, Shinji explores alternate versions of reality and ultimately chooses to affirm his own existence — concluding with all characters congratulating him. The abstract ending shocked audiences and remains one of anime's most discussed finales.

Note: The theatrical film The End of Evangelion (1997) provides an alternate, more explicit conclusion to the series, depicting the events of episodes 25 and 26 from an external perspective.
